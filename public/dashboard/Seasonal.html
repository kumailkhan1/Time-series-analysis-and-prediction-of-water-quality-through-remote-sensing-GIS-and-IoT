Canadian Water Quality Index Winter 2015-2020
Analysis

The highest value of water quality index recorded from 2015-2020 in Summer was 79.1 and the lowest was 18.7. Overall, most of the water quality was recorded in the range of 40 to 70 with an average value of 54.40810565, and the median and mode of 50.25 and 47.5, respectively.
Water Quality index recorded in Winter was better than that in Summer with a maximum value of 100 and average value of 56.9819, mode of 51.1 and median of 53.6.

Temperature(°C) Winter 2015-2020
Analysis

The highest value of temperature recorded from 2015-2020 in Summer was 30.15 C and the lowest was 9 C. Overall, the average temperature was 21.71.
The temperature recorded in Winter was lower than that in Summer with a maximum value of 29 C and average value of 18.466. The reason is overall difference between atmospheric changes in temperature in the two seasons.

Turbidity(NTU) Summer 2015-2020
Turbidity(NTU) Winter 2015-2020
Analysis

The highest value of turbidity recorded from 2015-2020 in Summer was 542.81 NTU. Overall, the average turbidity was 50.75299 NTU.
The turbidity recorded in Winter was quite lower than that in Summer with a maximum value of 353.0038 NTU and average value of 31.26899 NTU. The reason is that there is more rainfall and winds in summer as compared to than in Winter. The monsoon season also falls under the Summer-time period due to which the turbidity recorded in Summer is way more than in Winter.

pH Winter 2015-2020

Dissolved Solids(mg/l) Winter 2015-2020
Analysis

The number of dissolved solids in Summer was relatively higher than that in Winter with an average value of 285.75 and 278.786 mg/l respectively. The highest value recorded was 400 and 393.56 mg/l respectively. The reason is more rainfall and higher temperature in Summer as compared to Winter.
