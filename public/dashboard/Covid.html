Effect of Covid'19 on Rawal Lake Water Quality
Before Covid: March-Dec 2019 | During Covid: March-Dec 2020
  • Covid Analysis
Daily Canadian Water Quality Index 2019-2020
Analysis

The water quality index decreased during the covid period as compared to the one before with a maximum value of 76.7 and 79.1 respectively. The average water quality index recorded before covid was 61.3 and during covid was 52.4.

Daily Temperature(Â°C) 2019-2020
Analysis

The temperature remained almost same in before and during covid time period with a maximum value of 301.15 and 26.9 C and average of 22.12 and 22.29 C.

Daily Turbidity(NTU) 2019-2020
Analysis

The turbidity of water is significantly increased over the years especially in 2019 where it hit the peak with the value of 389.094 NTU. Overall, the daily value of turbidity of 2019 and 2020 is greater than that of previous years.

Daily pH 2019-2020
Analysis

The value of pH remains consistent over the span of 8 years. The highest pH was recorded in 2016 with a value of 13.5.

Daily Dissolved Oxygen(mg/l) 2019-2020
Analysis

The value of dissolved oxygen in water has significantly increased in the years 2018, 2019 and 2020 with the maximum value of 17.313 in 2018.

Daily Dissolved Solids(mg/l) 2019-2020
Analysis

The number of dissolved solids in water increased in the years 2015 and 2016 but decreased in the year 2017 and remained almost consistent afterwards. The maximum value recorded was 420 mg/l in 2014.
