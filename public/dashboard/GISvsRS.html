Analysis

The water quality index calculated from Geographic Information System (GIS) dataset is quite consistent as compared to than obtained from remote sensing (RS) dataset over the period of 31st Jan 2016-June 2020. The highest value recorded was 100 for both datasets and the lowest value was 17.3 and 29.1, respectively. The variance between the water quality index obtained from GIS and RS was 127.62 and 143.08 respectively.
The major reason of inconsistency in water quality index calculated from RS dataset is that value of water quality parameters obtained from RS have lower temporal resolution. Thus, the data has been interpolated to obtain the parameters for each day that affects the results.
Secondly, the data obtained from images of satellites depends on spatial resolution, atmospheric factors, cloudiness, satellite sensors etc. So, it is relatively less accurate than that obtained on ground. For example, if the weather is cloudy, the image is going to get affected that will cause the parameters to be less accurate and eventually end up variating the results.
